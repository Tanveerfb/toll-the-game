# TAO — Character Design Sheet

## Concept
An elderly martial arts master built from two characters who share the same trope: the unassuming old man who is secretly one of the most dangerous fighters alive. Tao's two modes aren't separate forms — they're the same person, shifting completely depending on the room he's in.

---

## Mode 1: Serious / Business / Battle (Netero-inspired)

### Look
- Trimmed, neat beard and mustache, hair tied back short and tidy — the "cleaned up for business" version of his usual look, mirroring Netero's appearance shift going into the Chimera Ant arc.
- Stretched, pierced earlobes (a holdover detail from his usual look, kept even when he tidies up).
- Physique visibly tightens and bulks — the same deceptively frail old frame fills out with dense, overwhelming muscle, echoing Roshi's Max Power shift. The message: the harmless-looking old man you weren't worried about is suddenly very worth worrying about.

### Outfit
- In combat: a simple, plain shirt and track shorts — function over form, nothing flashy.
- In formal/business settings: a structured robe or kimono carrying a visible mark of rank (insignia, sash, or emblem) — a quiet symbol of mastery that signals decades of discipline before he's said a word.

### Personality
- Calm, composed, and unnervingly controlled. The warmth disappears and is replaced by total focus.
- Commands a room or a fight through presence alone — doesn't need volume or theatrics.
- Precise and decisive; everything he does looks effortless, which is its own kind of intimidating.

---

## Mode 2: Chill / Goofball (Roshi-inspired)

### Look
- Sunglasses, loose beach-casual or robe-like clothing, a walking stick, relaxed and slouched posture.
- Beard and hair left messier and untidy compared to Mode 1 — visually "off duty."

### Personality
- Easygoing, warm, genuinely enjoys the people around him.
- Becomes a bit of a goofball around company he likes — teasing, lighthearted, unserious.
- Teacher energy: patient, a little mischievous, makes it easy to forget how dangerous he actually is.
- Carries a well-recognized status as a legendary fighter, but wears that reputation lightly — he's not trying to prove anything in this mode.

---

## The Throughline
The switch isn't a costume change, it's a mood change. Around people he trusts, in downtime, teaching, relaxing — he's the goofball. The moment work, business, or a real fight starts, all of that drops instantly and what's underneath is the serious, terrifyingly composed master. The contrast between the two is the entire point of the character.
